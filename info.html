1) A static analysis method for Ethereum smart contracts was proposed in the study. Topological analysis of function invocation relationships, logic risk detection, and location are the two major functions. We can find logic hazards to individual functions using a combination of syntax analysis and logic risk analysis. We created the SASC tool based on these characteristics. It can ensure that Ethereum smart contracts are of good quality.


2) Based on a smart contract on the Ethereum blockchain, we demonstrate a Decentralised App (DAPP) for the sharing of ordinary things. This contract allows customers to register and rent devices without the help of a Trusted Third Party (TTP), without disclosing any personal information, and without having to sign up for the service beforehand.
